1.5 Describe an “Ultimate Level” of HCI

I love getting the opportunity to talk about what I think is the ultimate level of HCI, the neural interface. Neural interfaces are also known as brain-computer interfaces (BCI), or brain-machine interfaces (BMI). A neural interface is a device that is hooked up to the brain that allows the user to interact with different external devices, such as computers, robotics and hearing aids.

The most common use of neural interface is the cochlear implant which aids people who have hearing issues. There are many more uses for neural interfaces in development such as restoring vision or nerve damage, and military use for soldiers.

BCI’s use many different methods to read electrical signals from the user’s brain and translate them to a given task they are programmed to do. Some of these methods are more effective than others. Neural lace a method being developed by Neuralink involves implanting electrodes into the hosts brain via surgery. These implanted electrodes are able to gather neural signals much more effectively than any external signal reader.

Neuralink has been in the headlines recently for its radical new advancements in neural interfaces. The company was founded in 2016 and is owned by billionaire Elon Musk. Neuralinks higher bandwidth devices opens up many doors previously impossible.

Privacy is a big concern when it comes to neural interfaces. Imagine a company having access to your thoughts allowing hyper-targeted advertising. Even worse someone is able to maliciously access your private information via some vulnerability in the interface. As these technologies grow, so too will the ethical issues surrounding them.

I believe neural interfaces are the future of human interaction with computers. I think we will eventually live in a world similar to William Gibsons Neuromancer, but hopefully not as dystopian. In Neuromancer users can connect to cyberspace via their rig using some cybernetic implants.
