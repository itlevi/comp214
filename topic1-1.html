1.1 Create a Timeline of Computer Interface Technology

Punch cards are the earliest of the human computer interfaces. They were originally used to control looms. Allowing the efficient automation of fabric patterns, which varied depending on the location of the holes on the cards. Punch cards evolved and were later used in the 1890 US census. IMB adopted the use of punch cards and were widely used until the late 1950s.

The QWERTY keyboard was invented by Christopher Latham Sholes and his two friends Carlos Glidden and Samuel W. Soulé. Prior to the QWERTY style keyboard, typewriter keys were arranged in a piano type layout. Christopher was trying to design a more efficient way to type as early typewriters often had mechanical failings due to their key layout. QWERTY style typewriters became widely popular and are still the standard modern keyboard layout.

An earlier version of the trackball was invented by Ralph Benjamin for the military in 1946. Another version of the trackball was later invented by Tom Cranston, Fred Longstaff and Kenyon Taylor in 1952. The Canadian team used a 5-pin bowling ball and a scanner to map the area to the screen. Trackball mice are still in production today and are used in some ergonomic mice.

The Atari CX40 was the first widely used digital joystick. The CX40 was the newer and more widely produced version of the original CX10. The ability to be used on other platforms other than the Atari aided in the popularity of the CX40.

The NEW Zapper was a gun accessory for the Nintendo Entertainment System. Pressing the trigger on the gun would cause the television screen to become black for a single frame. During the next frame all of the valid targets would become white. The gun would detect changed in light levels to determine if the target was hit or missed.

The Wii remote or Wiimote was a video game controller released with the Nintendo Wii. It had a motion sensor, accelerometer and optical sensor. It used all of these combined to recognize gestures and detect where the device was pointing. It also had Bluetooth capabilities to wireless connect to the Wii console. The unique Wii remote was in production for almost five years.

The Microsoft Kinect was released in 2010 for the Xbox 360 gaming console. It used RGB cameras, infrared cameras and microphones to map depth. The device could detect real-time gestures. It also had speech recognition and skeletal detection. It allowed multiple users to use hands free user interfaces to interact with the game console. The technology was later adapted by Microsoft for user in academic, robotics and other non-gaming activities.

The Oculus rift is a next-generation virtual reality headset. Initially developed as a cheaper and more accessible alternative to competing headsets. Modern VR systems use a combination of infrared, motion capture, gyroscopes and accelerometers. The headset allows the user to experience virtual worlds in a full 360-degree environment.

Apple glass is an augmented reality eyewear currently in development by Apple. Augmented reality in the newest iteration in human computer interaction. AR takes the real world and creates an interactive experience by augmenting visual and audio aspects of it. AR heads up displays use eye tracking to interact with menus and can even completely render new environments on the fly.
