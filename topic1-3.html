1.3 Man versus Technology

I have always been intrigued by the idea of artificial intelligence. The idea that a computer can eventually become as intelligent as a human is something has some people concerned. Both Steven Hawking and Elon Musk have erred on the side of caution, when it comes to the future and AI. So much so that they and others have signed a document warning other of these potential dangers.

The hypothetical scenario I am referring to is an AI becoming so advanced that it uses its supreme intelligence and capabilities to take over the planet and humans alongside of it. An AI taking over the world is the ultimate scenario when it comes to Man Vs. Technology. It is the cumulation of all previous battles.

We can already feel the negative impact of advancements in artificial intelligence. Intelligent robots have already taken over countless manufacturing jobs. Manufacturing jobs have been on decline since 1980. Robots are also working their way into hospitals and surgery rooms. Self-driving cars and transports could eliminate the need to drivers, as they do not need to sleep, or lose focus while fatigued.

I personally haven’t had an issue with technology, or any issues with an advancement in the foreseeable future. I have spent most of my adult life working with technology and I plan on doing so until my retirement. I try not to fight with technology and instead look for ways that it can enrich my life and the lives of my family.

People often fear things they don’t understand. When a non-technical person thinks of AI, they already associate it with something negative. They don’t fully or even partially understand what an AI is, so they automatically associate it with something they have seen in mainstream media. Personally speaking, I cannot wait for our benevolent AI overlords.
