3.3 Describe a Specialized Gaming Interface

Modern VR systems use a combination of headset and controller to provide a surreal human-computer interaction. The headset delivers the auditory and visual feedback to the user and the motion controllers allow the user to interact with objects the virtual world. Some headsets include a microphone for communication with other users in online games, and to use voice commands. Most VR systems two motion controllers, one for each hand.

The VR headset also known as the head-mounted display (HMD) contains two small digital screens that each display their own image which when combined produce stereoscopic images. The headset contains gyroscopes and motion detectors to position the viewport, camera and position within the virtual world.

The motions controllers that come with the HTC Vive, in particular, provide the user with haptic feedback, also known as 3D touch. Haptic feedback is the process of outputting vibrations that create the feeling of touching an object.

VR systems are not only used for entertainment. The equipment and technology are used in medical, education, engineering and military applications. It can provide doctors with surgical training at a reduced cost with no lives put at risk.

Having used a new VR system recently I can say the experience was quite amazing. It is a truly remarkable advancement in human-computer interaction. My young children who are 3, 7 and 11 all tried it as well. The children were completely immersed in the world and I can only imagine what that experience would have been if I had access to this technology when I was their age. After watching them play for a while I saw how potentially hazardous it can be, especially if you don’t have a dedicated space free of clutter, sharp furniture or tripping hazards.

One thing I did notice while using the VR headset is that if you have poor vision the headsets optical adjuster cannot adjust enough to compensate, which results in a blurry experience.
