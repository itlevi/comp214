1.2 Profile an HCI Developer

Jeff Raskin was a human computer interaction expert who worked for Apple and has been attributed with the conception of the original Macintosh computer. In 1967 he graduated from Pennsylvania State University with a degree in computer science. Jeff passed away in 2005.

Raskin developed his own programming language called FLOW while working as an assistant professor at the University of California. The language only had several statements and could not interact with numbers. FLOW was primarily used to teach programming to non-technical students.

Jeff started working for Apple in the very early days of the company. He began his work by creating BASIC programming manuals. In 1978 computers were not as widespread and ubiquitous as they are today. He saw the a future where personal computers were everywhere and would be able to do so much more. Jeff is best known for his contributions to the early development of the Macintosh. The Macintosh was designed to be a low cost, highly functional and easily accessible computer.

After leaving Apple in 1982 he created the company Information Appliance, Inc which was responsible for creation of the Canon CAT. The CAT is often referred to as the spiritual heir of the original Macintosh.

In 2000 Jeff published his book The Human Interface, which contains suggestions for human computer interface design. The book also contains some interesting rules that he recommends when designing interfaces.

Jeff spent over 30 years building a new human computer interface called The Human Environment, which he later renamed to Archy. His son Aza Raskin has continued the work on Archy after Jeffs passing.
